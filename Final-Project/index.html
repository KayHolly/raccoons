Cooper's Hill Cheese-Rolling and Wake


What is the Cheese-Rolling Race?

Every spring, a little hill in Gloucestershire, England transforms into the world’s most delightfully unhinged sporting arena. Welcome to the Cooper's Hill Cheese-Rolling and Wake, where the goal is simple: chase a nine-pound wheel of Double Gloucester cheese down a ridiculously steep hill... and try not to break any bones on the way down. Competitors from around the globe—ranging from trained athletes to brave (or slightly bonkers) amateurs—fling themselves down the 200-yard slope after the runaway cheese, which can reach speeds up to 70 mph. Spoiler alert: you’re not catching the cheese. But the first person to cross the finish line wins it, along with eternal bragging rights and possibly a few bruises. Spectators come for the chaos, the camaraderie, and the sheer absurdity of it all. It’s muddy, it’s messy, it’s slightly mad—and that’s exactly why it’s legendary. Part tradition, part adrenaline rush, and 100% British eccentricity, the Cooper's Hill Cheese-Rolling is not for the faint of heart—but it's unforgettable for everyone involved.


Is it dangerous?

Oh, dangerous? No, not at all—just a bunch of people hurling themselves down a near-vertical hill after a speeding wheel of cheese. What could possibly go wrong? Broken bones, dislocated joints, concussions? Mere souvenirs! It’s basically a gentle stroll—if that stroll involved gravity doing unspeakable things to your body. Totally safe. Bring grandma.
